# Getting Comfortable Using AI
## Revised Course Outline — Three Sessions, One Shipped App

**Format:** In-person or virtual · 5 hours total · 20 students max  
**Schedule:** Can be delivered as a single full day or split across multiple days — always 3 sessions  
**Tools:** Google AI Studio · Gemini · Claude · ChatGPT · Cloudinary  
**Outcome:** Every student leaves knowing how to work with AI tools — crafting prompts to generate real output — with a live, published web presence as proof of what they built

---

## Course Philosophy

Students describe their idea at the start of the day and share a live link at the end. Every tool and technique learned is applied immediately to their own project — nothing goes into a folder to "use later." The portfolio *is* the work from minute one.

AI will surprise you. It will also disappoint you. Both are part of learning. This course is about honest experimentation, not performance.

---

## Pre-Work (sent to students before the day)

- Think of a project, cause, or personal interest you'd like a simple web page for — a portfolio, a community resource, a small business, a passion project
- Bring 2–3 real photos and one short description of yourself or your work (even rough notes are fine)
- Create free accounts: **Google AI Studio**, **Gemini**, **Claude.ai**, **Cloudinary**
- Optional: bring a phone so you can preview your site on mobile

## Session 1 — "I Built a Website Today" · 2 hours

**Goal:** Every student has a live URL by the end of this session. The "wow" moment comes first — but we lay the foundation first.

### Part 1 — AI Foundations (20 min)

A quick, plain-language orientation to the terms students will hear all day. No jargon left unexplained.

| Term | What it means in plain English |
|------|-------------------------------|
| **AI (Artificial Intelligence)** | Software that can do things we used to think only humans could do — like write, draw, answer questions, and make decisions |
| **Machine Learning** | How AI gets smarter — by studying millions of examples rather than being hand-programmed with rules |
| **Generative AI (GenAI)** | AI that *creates* new things — text, images, audio, video, code — rather than just analyzing what already exists |
| **Prompt** | The instruction or question you give an AI. The quality of what you get back is directly shaped by how clearly you ask |
| **Vibe Coding** | Describing what you want to build in plain English and letting AI write the code for you — no programming experience required |

**Key message to open with:** You don't need to understand how AI works under the hood any more than you need to understand how an engine works to drive a car. What matters is knowing how to steer it.

Invite 2–3 students to share what they've already heard about AI — good or bad. Acknowledge both. This is a judgment-free room.

### Part 2 — Live Demo (20 min)

Instructor walks through the exact flow students will use — no mystery, no magic:

1. Start with a one-paragraph brief written in Claude: *"I'm building a landing page for [project]. My audience is [who]. The tone should feel [adjective]. I want sections for: hero, about, work/services, and contact."*
2. Paste the brief into Google AI Studio → watch a site appear
3. Critique it openly: *"What works? What got weird?"*
   - Hero sections: usually strong
   - Copy: often generic — we'll fix that
   - Images: placeholders — we'll fix that too
4. Show one quick chat refinement: *"Make the hero section bolder and cut the text by half"*

**Key message:** The first output is never the final output. Iteration is the skill.

### Part 3 — Students Build Site #1 (45 min)

Students work in **pairs** (reduces anxiety, increases problem-solving).

**Starter prompt template** — copy, customize, generate:

```
Create a landing page for [a photographer / writer / consultant / nonprofit / community organizer].
Use modern design with bold typography.
Include: hero section, about, work samples with placeholder images, and a contact form.
Tone: [professional / warm / bold / friendly].
```

Each student picks their persona or real project, generates, and shares screens with their pair.

Instructor circulates. Facilitators (if available) support pairs that get stuck.

**Debrief prompt for the room:** *"What surprised you? What got weird? What did AI get exactly right?"*

Capture responses — these become teaching material for Part 3.

### Part 4 — Pattern Recognition + The 3 Prompt Levers (30 min)

Draw on what the room just discovered:

- **What AI consistently nails:** layout, structure, section flow, visual hierarchy
- **What AI consistently misses:** your actual voice, specific details, real content
- **The 3 levers that change everything:**
  1. **Specificity** — vague prompts get generic results; specific prompts get useful ones
  2. **Tone** — "professional" and "warm and conversational" produce completely different sites
  3. **Structure requests** — you can ask for exactly what you want: *"3-column grid," "no sidebar," "one bold headline only"*

**Session 1 milestone:** ✅ Every student has a generated site and understands why the first draft isn't the last draft.

**Between-session prompt** *(for virtual cohorts with a break between days, or as a lunch task):*  
*"Decide on your real project. Write your one-paragraph brief in Claude before Session 2."*

## Session 2 — "I Made It Mine" · 2 hours

**Goal:** Students iterate their site into something genuinely theirs — real copy, real images, real personality, and a voice or music layer using Gemini.

### Part 1 — Before/After Demo (20 min)

Instructor shows the same site from Session 1, then walks through 4 refinements in real time — including one audio moment:

1. *"Make the hero section full-width with a single bold headline. Remove the subtitle."*
2. *"Change the color palette to deep navy and warm white. No gradients."*
3. *"Rewrite the about section in first person. Make it sound like a real human, not a brochure."*
4. Use Gemini TTS to generate a short spoken intro; play it for the room — point out how fast and how personal it sounds

**Key message:** You are the creative director. The AI is the production team. Tell it what you want — and it can produce more than just visuals.

### Part 2 — Deep Iteration Workshop (90 min)

**Before the workshop begins — Cloudinary setup (5 min):**

Introduce Cloudinary as the class asset library — a free cloud storage account where students upload their generated images and audio so the app can reliably display and play them.

- Each student creates a free Cloudinary account (or instructor provides a shared class account)
- Walk through the two actions they'll use: **upload a file** and **copy the URL**
- That URL is what gets pasted into their site — images, voice intros, music tracks all work the same way

*Frame it simply: "Cloudinary is your creative storage locker. Everything you make today lives there so your site can find it from anywhere."*

---

Students work individually on their real project. Structured to prevent stalling.

**The 7-Refinement Card** *(printed or shared digitally — students work through in order; audio steps are not optional):*

| # | Focus | Example prompt to try |
|---|-------|-----------------------|
| 1 | **Layout & structure** | *"Move the contact form to its own section at the bottom. Add a pricing section between work and contact."* |
| 2 | **Visual & tone** | *"Switch to a warm earthy palette — terracotta, cream, and dark brown. Make it feel handmade and personal."* |
| 3 | **Copy & voice** | *"Rewrite the hero headline to feel more direct and confident. Give me 3 options."* |
| 4 | **Add a missing piece** | *"Add a FAQ section with 4 questions relevant to [my work]."* |
| 5 | **Images** | Use Gemini (Nano Banana) to generate 2–3 custom images → upload to Cloudinary → paste the URL into the site |
| 6 | **Voice narration** | Use Gemini TTS to record a 20–30 second spoken intro → upload to Cloudinary → embed or link it on the site |
| 7 | **Background music** | Use Gemini to generate a short ambient music track → upload to Cloudinary → embed or link it on the site |

**A note on audio:** Gemini makes voice and music generation surprisingly quick — students often finish both in under 10 minutes combined. Frame it as: *"You just recorded and scored a website. Without a studio."*

**Facilitator cues:**
- If a student is stuck on refinement 1 after 15 minutes → skip to refinement 3 (copy is faster)
- If a student finishes all 7 early → bonus: generate a second music track in a completely different mood and compare
- If a student's site "broke" → treat it as a teaching moment; ask AI to fix it in chat
- Audio refinements (6 & 7) are the most delightful — save them as a reward for students who are anxious about the earlier steps

### Part 3 — Lightning Gallery (30 min)

Students paste their live URL into a shared doc or channel. No screen-sharing scramble.

Pick **4–5 volunteers** (or instructor picks interesting examples) for a brief live showcase:
- *"What's one prompt that changed everything for you?"*
- *"What did AI get wrong that you had to fix?"*
- *"Play your voice intro or music for the room"* — at least one audio showcase

Class votes on: best hero, most improved, most surprising output, best voice or music moment.

**Session 2 milestone:** ✅ Site has the student's real voice, real images, real audio, and a structure they chose — not what the AI defaulted to.

## Session 3 — "I Shipped It" · 1 hour

**Goal:** Polish, publish, present, reflect.

### Part 1 — Final Polish (15 min)

Each student picks their **top 2 remaining fixes** — keep it focused, not open-ended:

- Mobile check: preview on phone or browser dev tools; fix anything obviously broken
- Add one real piece of content: a real photo, a real testimonial, a real project description
- Write a real page title and meta description (use Claude: *"Write a compelling page title and meta description for my site about [project]"*)

### Part 2 — Publishing (15 min)

Walk through the exact Google AI Studio publishing flow together as a group.

**Facilitator note:** Pre-test this end-to-end before the day. Know where friction points are and have a fallback ready. If publishing fails for anyone, a shareable preview link or exported screenshot is a valid artifact — the work is real regardless of the URL.

Each student shares their link (or screenshot) in the group channel.

### Part 3 — Demo Day (20 min)

8 students × ~2.5 minutes each (adjust based on cohort size):

- **90 seconds:** "Here's what I built and the one prompt that made the biggest difference"
- **60 seconds:** "Here's what broke — and how I fixed it (or what I'd try next)"

No slides. Just the live site and honest reflection.

### Part 4 — Debrief + What's Next (10 min)

**What AI is genuinely good at:**
- Speed — a functional layout in minutes
- Structure — sections, hierarchy, flow
- Options — generate 3 versions and pick the best one
- Fixing itself — ask it to correct what it got wrong

**What still needs you:**
- Your actual story, voice, and content
- Judgment about what looks right and what doesn't
- Real photos, real testimonials, real specifics

**Where to go next (optional self-paced tracks):**
- Deeper audio: explore ElevenLabs for more voice customization, or Suno for more music styles
- Video: Kling AI or Veo 3 for a short background or intro clip
- Going deeper on the site: Lovable or v0 for more complex app features and interactivity

**Certificate moment:** Walk through the digital badge and certificate process. Students who completed all 3 sessions and have a published (or screenshot) site earn their credential.

**Session 3 milestone:** ✅ Student has a live link, has presented their work, and can articulate what they'd do next.

## Hardware Requirements

**Each student needs:**
- A laptop or desktop computer (Windows, Mac, or Chromebook) with a modern browser (Chrome recommended)
- Stable internet connection — generation tools are bandwidth-intensive; plan for at least 10 Mbps per student
- A microphone (built-in is fine) for voice narration in Session 2
- A phone (optional but recommended) for mobile previewing in Session 3

**Instructor / facilitator needs:**
- Laptop with screen share capability
- External display or projector visible to all students
- Backup WiFi hotspot in case venue connection degrades under load

**Venue / virtual platform needs:**
- For in-person: reliable WiFi with enough bandwidth for 20 simultaneous users hitting generative AI APIs
- For virtual: Zoom, Google Meet, or similar with screen share and breakout room support

**Not required:** Any software installation, coding environment, or prior accounts (accounts are created at the start of the course or in pre-work)

## Facilitation Notes

**Scheduling options:**
- **Single full day:** Sessions 1, 2, and 3 run back-to-back with breaks between. Best for cohort energy and momentum.
- **Multi-day split:** Any combination works — e.g., Session 1 on Day 1, Sessions 2 and 3 on Day 2, or one session per day across three days. When splitting, send a between-session prompt to keep students thinking about their project (see end of Session 1).

**For virtual delivery:**
- Use breakout rooms for pair work in Session 1
- Shared Google Doc or Slack channel for URL gallery in Session 2
- Main room for demos and debrief

**For in-person delivery:**
- Print the 7-Refinement Card for every student
- Have a backup WiFi hotspot — generation tools are bandwidth-hungry
- Walk the room during iteration blocks; stalled students often won't ask for help

**The most important facilitation move:** When something goes wrong (AI generates something weird, a site breaks, a student hates their output) — **celebrate it openly**. These moments teach more than the successes do.

## Certification Requirements

To earn the Certificate of Completion and digital badge:

- ✅ Attend and participate in all 3 sessions
- ✅ Complete at least 7 chat refinements on a real project (including at least one audio output)
- ✅ Submit a published site link or screenshot portfolio
- ✅ Present work during Demo Day
- ✅ Provide peer feedback during the lightning gallery
